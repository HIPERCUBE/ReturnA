“Twinkle, twinkle, little star,” how I wonder where you
are. Wonder no more. San Diego has always been an
excellent place to spot the stars, with its quiet hilltops and
plains. “Star light, star bright,” it all awaits at the Palomar
Observatory tonight. The Palomar Observatory, located on
Palomar Mountain, offers a view of sparkles in the sky with
five telescopes used nightly for a wide variety of
astronomical research. It is home of the famous Hale
telescope, a 200­inch telescope that is the workhorse for
modern astronomy.
