Language translation was an early goal of computer
science, but there is still a long way to go. The reason is
simple: It doesn’t always work. Translation errors occur due
to the complex features of computers, languages and minds.
Even with an excellent dictionary and complete grammar
knowledge of both languages, machine translation is often
inaccurate. The meaning of a sentence is not often fully
contained in its words. There is something else, called
context, that a speaker and a listener (or a writer and a
reader) bring to language to get its exact meaning. Without
the context, accurate computer translation is often not
possible.
