41.  I was once a professional violinist. I’ve played on many 
violins, and they all respond differently. The key, I’ve 
discovered, to releasing an instrument’s most beautiful sound is 
to know it so well that you simply understand what it responds to 
and what it doesn’t; which strings need tenderness and which 
withstand force; how fast or hard to draw the bow. How one 
violin responds won’t necessarily be like any other. Each has its 
own personality. People respond the same way. Their beauty 
needs to be released, not extracted, and the only way to know how 
they respond is to listen carefully and behold their true nature.
