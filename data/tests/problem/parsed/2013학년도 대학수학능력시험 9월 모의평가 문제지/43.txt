43. Studying history is not about memorizing what we 
have been told― it requires us to investigate the 
past. Like a detective, we start with the easy, known 
pieces of information.
(A) You have to go further to ask questions such as, “Why 
was he cruel?” and “What were the results of his rule?” 
Hence, studying history trains us not to accept everything 
we read or hear as the truth. Instead, it trains us to use our 
critical thinking skills to get the full picture of the past.
