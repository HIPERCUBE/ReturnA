similar conclusion comes from research on combat veterans.
Going through combat is a highly stressful, dangerous, and
terrifying experience, marked by death of friends and
uncertainty about one’s own survival. Yet military groups
who experience combat seem to bond with each other from
the experience. One sign of this is that military reunions are
better attended by groups who went through combat than by
groups who did not share battlefield experience.
