38. When writers want to convince people of something, they
generally rely on two means of persuasion : appealing to the
reader’s sense of logic and appealing to the reader’s emotions.
When writers rely only on appeals to emotion, they neglect to
provide any real evidence for why readers should believe what
they say. Writers who rely solely on emotional appeals usually
hope to get their readers so angry, scared, or excited that
they will forget to look for reason or sense in the argument.
Unfortunately, many readers aren’t aware of this strategy, so
they may accept arguments that are unfounded, misleading, or
both.
① 충분한 근거를 제시하는 글이 독자들에게 더 인기가 많다.
② 잘 쓰여진 글은 모든 세대의 공감을 얻는다.
③ 글의 종류에 따라 쓰기 전략이 달라진다.
④ 독자의 감정 상태에 따라 글의 해석이 달라질 수 있다.
⑤ 감정에 호소하는 글은 독자의 판단력을 흐리게 할 수 있다.
