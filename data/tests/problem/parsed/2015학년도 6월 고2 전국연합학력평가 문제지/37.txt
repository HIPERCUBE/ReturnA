The need for touch may seem like common sense. ( ① ) 
However, in the early 1900s, people in Europe believed that 
touching newborns was not good for them and they thought 
that it would spread germs and make the babies weak and 
whiny. ( ② ) In the orphanages at that time, it was not 
permitted to cuddle newborn babies. ( ③ ) The babies were 
well fed and cared for, but many of them became ill. ( ④ ) 
The sick babies began to get better gradually. ( ⑤ ) Recent 
research that has confirmed the importance of touch for 
babies encourages parents and nurses to touch and stroke 
premature babies as much as possible.
