32. It is around the age of ten or eleven that most children 
take on music as a real interest. By the age of eighteen or 
twenty, most people form their tastes for music. We seek 
out different kinds of music, and the choice of music has 
important social consequences. We listen to the music that 
our friends listen to. In search of our identity, we form 
bonds or social groups with people whom we want to be 
like. As a way of externalizing the bond, we listen to the 
same kind of music, and music becomes a mark of 
personal and group identity and of distinction.
