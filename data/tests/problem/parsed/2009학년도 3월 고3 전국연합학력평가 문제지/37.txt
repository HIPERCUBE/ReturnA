37. snowy plover에 관한 다음 글의 내용과 일치하지 않는 것은?
The snowy plover is one of the hardest birds to spot on
the beach. Once numbering in the thousands, the species is
listed as endangered in several states now. Beige and white,
snowy plovers blend perfectly in the white sand. They eat
tiny flies that live on the sand or mud flats. Their nests
usually contain three tiny eggs, which look like sand and are
barely visible to even the most well­trained eye. Mothers
incubate the nests during the day and fathers at night.
Chicks can get out of their nest within three hours of
hatching. They walk, run, and swim well but still require
their parents’ care after hatching.
