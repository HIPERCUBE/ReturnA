28. Some kinds of spiders weave thick white bands of silk
across the centers of their webs. The purpose of these extra
bands is to . The bands
make the webs many times more visible. Scientists
discovered the purpose of the extra bands while observing
the spiders in Florida. They noticed that songbirds would
change their flight directions suddenly just before flying into
clearly marked webs. The white bands seemed to serve as
markers to warn off birds that might tear the webs. The
birds themselves benefited from noticing the webs. Flying
into the webs left their feathers covered with sticky threads.
The same phenomenon was observed in the case of
butterflies and other large insects as well.
