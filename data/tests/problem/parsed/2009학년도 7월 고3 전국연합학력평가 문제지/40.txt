Drug companies spend almost a year to get ready for the
flu season. That’s because it takes about nine months to
make all the vaccine that will be given out in flu shots.
(A) The virus in the egg white is treated with a chemical so
it won’t get people sick. Several strains of the virus are
mixed into one vaccine, processed and then shipped out to
doctors.
(B) Once the strains are chosen, the virus is injected into
fertilized chicken eggs to grow. After several months,
technicians remove the egg white, which contains the flu
virus.
(C) Making the vaccine for millions of flu shots isn’t easy.
There are many strains of the flu, so the first step is
deciding which strains to vaccinate against.
*strain: 변종

③ (B) - (C) - (A) ④ (C) - (A)- (B)
