24. As soon as we are born, the world gets to work on us
and transforms us from merely biological into social units.
Every human being at every stage of history or pre­history
is born into a society and from his earliest years is formed
by that society. The language which he speaks, for example,
is not an individual inheritance, but a social acquisition from
the group in which he grows up. Both language and
environment help to determine the character of his thought;
his earliest ideas come to him from others. As has been
