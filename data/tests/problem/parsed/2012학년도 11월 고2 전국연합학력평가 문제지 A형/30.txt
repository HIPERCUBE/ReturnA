The graph above shows the percentage of smartphone owners 
in the United States that reported doing the listed activities on 
their phone in January 2011 and January 2012. ① The primary 
use for over 70% of owners in both 2011 and 2012 was 
texting. ② The biggest gap in use between the two years, 
which comes to 7%, is seen in using apps. ③ The percentages 
of users playing games in both years are the same, accounting 
for 49%. ④ Except for updating social networks, the percentages 
of all the listed activities are more than 40% in 2012. ⑤ Using 
phones for coupons shows a decrease in activity from 14% in 
2011 to 9% in 2012.
